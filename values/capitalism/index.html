Capitalism

Category: Social

An economic system where individuals own businesses and property, and prices are determined by competition in the market.

Why this matters

Capitalism matters when "An economic system where individuals own businesses and property, and prices are determined by competition in the market" becomes a practice someone can return to under pressure. As a social value, it reaches beyond individual preference. One signal is the choice to invest when a more automatic reaction would be easier. The goal is to make capitalism concrete enough that another person could recognize the value in motion.

Examples in action

  • The entrepreneur embodied capitalism by investing her savings to start a small business, taking risks, and responding to market demands.
  • Capitalism can also appear through its neighboring values, such as Legacy, Money, and Sports, when the situation asks someone to invest with more intention and precision.
  • Capitalism becomes practical when someone can point to one behavior, such as "invest", and explain why that behavior fits the moment.

How to practice

  • Make the value visible to one other person through a choice they could actually notice.
  • Write down the smallest possible version of the action behind "compete" and use it to resist individual preference.
  • Pick one verb from the map, such as "build", and use it in a situation small enough to finish today.
  • Look for the place where capitalism is being admired but not enacted, then choose the smallest useful action.

Frequently asked questions

What does capitalism mean as a value?
To value capitalism is to let An economic system where individuals own businesses and property, and prices are determined by competition in the market guide behavior. The clearest clues are usually actions like invest, compete, and build.
How do I practice capitalism?
Practice capitalism by choosing one action connected to "invest" and making it observable today. The goal is a real behavior, not a perfect description of the value.
What values are related to capitalism?
Related values include Legacy, Money, and Sports. Looking at them together can show whether the moment is asking for capitalism, something adjacent, or a blend of several values.
