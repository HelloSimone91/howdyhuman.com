Loyalty

Category: Interpersonal

Being loyal involves staying true to someone or something, supporting them through good times and bad.

Why this matters

Loyalty matters when "Being loyal involves staying true to someone or something, supporting them through good times and bad" has to become more than a sentence someone agrees with. It becomes real in the quality of attention someone brings to another person. It becomes concrete when someone chooses to commit and lets that action carry the value. That is where the value earns trust: in the gap between what someone says matters and what they actually do.

Examples in action

  • Despite receiving better job offers, she showed loyalty to the company that had supported her during difficult personal times.
  • Loyalty becomes practical when someone can point to one behavior, such as "commit", and explain why that behavior fits the moment.
  • When defensiveness starts to pull attention away from loyalty, the value becomes concrete by returning to "defend" as a next move.
