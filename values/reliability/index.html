Reliability

Category: Core Values

Being dependable and consistently doing what you say you will do.

Why this matters

Reliability becomes useful when "Being dependable and consistently doing what you say you will do" starts guiding real choices instead of staying in private intention. It helps define what kind of person, team, or community someone is becoming. This is why the verb map matters: fulfill, deliver, and complete make reliability easier to spot in real life. In that sense, reliability is less about claiming the word and more about making one observable choice.

Examples in action

  • The supplier demonstrated reliability by delivering materials on time for every order over five years, even during industry-wide shortages.
  • Instead of treating reliability as an identity label, someone might practice it by choosing the action behind "deliver", naming what it protects, and noticing what it interrupts.
  • Another example is choosing "complete" in a small but visible way before the value has become easy or automatic.

How to practice

  • Write down the smallest possible version of the action behind "deliver" and use it to resist convenience.
  • Ask what reliability would change in the next conversation, errand, decision, or repair.
  • Choose one related value, such as Achievement, Effectiveness, and Mission, and decide whether the moment needs that value, reliability, or both.
  • Use the phrase "Being dependable and consistently doing what you say you will do" as a check: what action would make that definition visible today?

Frequently asked questions

What does reliability mean as a value?
Reliability describes Being dependable and consistently doing what you say you will do. The important question is what someone does with that meaning when a real choice is in front of them.
How do I practice reliability?
Look for a place where reliability is already being tested. Use "deliver" as the next action, then notice what changed afterward.
What values are related to reliability?
Reliability shares territory with Achievement, Effectiveness, and Mission, but each value points attention toward a slightly different action.
