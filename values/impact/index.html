Impact

Category: Social

Making a positive difference in people's lives or the world around us.

Why this matters

Impact matters because the idea of "Making a positive difference in people's lives or the world around us" is only trustworthy when it changes behavior. It asks how choices affect a wider group and the systems people share. You can often see it in the decision to influence, especially when nobody is forcing that choice. This gives impact enough specificity to be noticed in a conversation, routine, decision, or repair.

Examples in action

  • The literacy program had tremendous impact, helping thousands of adults learn to read and significantly improving their employment opportunities and self-confidence.
  • When individual preference starts to pull attention away from impact, the value becomes concrete by returning to "change" as a next move.
  • When the moment is messy, impact may look like choosing to influence before the perfect language, timing, or permission arrives.

How to practice

  • Choose one related value, such as Charisma, Leadership, and Legacy, and decide whether the moment needs that value, impact, or both.
  • Compare impact with Charisma, Leadership, and Legacy; notice where they support each other and where they ask for different actions.
  • Reread the opening example and name the exact behavior that made impact visible.
  • Name the pressure that makes impact harder, then choose one action that answers that pressure directly.

Frequently asked questions

What does impact mean as a value?
Impact is not only a word for Making a positive difference in people's lives or the world around us; it is also a pattern of behavior shaped by actions like influence, change, transform, and shape.
How do I practice impact?
Make impact practical by pairing it with a small commitment: one choice to transform, one conversation to have, or one pattern to interrupt.
What values are related to impact?
Looking at Charisma, Leadership, and Legacy alongside impact can make the choice in front of someone more precise.
