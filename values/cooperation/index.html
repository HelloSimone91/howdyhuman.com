Cooperation

Category: Interpersonal

Working together toward shared goals.

Why this matters

Cooperation matters when "Working together toward shared goals" becomes a practice someone can return to under pressure. As an interpersonal value, it shapes the space between people. One signal is the choice to assist when a more automatic reaction would be easier. The goal is to make cooperation concrete enough that another person could recognize the value in motion.

Examples in action

  • The international climate initiative succeeded through cooperation between countries that put aside differences to address shared environmental challenges.
  • Cooperation can also appear through its neighboring values, such as Benevolence, Caring, and Compassion, when the situation asks someone to assist with more intention and precision.
  • Cooperation becomes practical when someone can point to one behavior, such as "assist", and explain why that behavior fits the moment.

How to practice

  • Make the value visible to one other person through a choice they could actually notice.
  • Write down the smallest possible version of the action behind "aid" and use it to resist defensiveness.
  • Pick one verb from the map, such as "help", and use it in a situation small enough to finish today.
  • Look for the place where cooperation is being admired but not enacted, then choose the smallest useful action.

Frequently asked questions

What does cooperation mean as a value?
To value cooperation is to let Working together toward shared goals guide behavior. The clearest clues are usually actions like assist, aid, and help.
How do I practice cooperation?
Practice cooperation by choosing one action connected to "assist" and making it observable today. The goal is a real behavior, not a perfect description of the value.
What values are related to cooperation?
Related values include Benevolence, Caring, and Compassion. Looking at them together can show whether the moment is asking for cooperation, something adjacent, or a blend of several values.
