Learning

Category: Growth

Gaining knowledge or skills through study, experience, or teaching.

Why this matters

Learning matters when "Gaining knowledge or skills through study, experience, or teaching" has to become more than a sentence someone agrees with. As a growth value, it asks for practice rather than performance. It becomes concrete when someone chooses to study and lets that action carry the value. That is where the value earns trust: in the gap between what someone says matters and what they actually do.

Examples in action

  • Even after 40 years in the field, the surgeon prioritized learning by attending conferences, reading research, and seeking feedback from colleagues.
  • In practice, learning can show up while someone is learning, revising, or trying again after feedback: someone starts with the action behind "study" instead of leaving the value as a private intention.
  • In a specific decision, learning becomes easier to recognize when the person can name what they are choosing to explore and what that action serves.

How to practice

  • Find one real situation while someone is learning, revising, or trying again after feedback, then choose one action connected to "study".
  • Notice where you talk about learning more easily than you practice it, then choose one concrete verb to close that gap.
  • Ask what learning would change in the next conversation, errand, decision, or repair.
  • Choose one related value, such as Exploration, Fine Art, and History, and decide whether the moment needs that value, learning, or both.

Frequently asked questions

What does learning mean as a value?
Learning means Gaining knowledge or skills through study, experience, or teaching. It becomes a lived value when someone uses actions like study, discover, explore, and research to make that meaning visible in a real situation.
How do I practice learning?
Start with a situation where the wish to already be good at it is likely to take over. Then choose a small action connected to "discover" that makes learning visible.
What values are related to learning?
Exploration, Fine Art, and History sit close to learning in the dictionary. They help frame the situations where this value becomes useful or complicated.
