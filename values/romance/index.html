Romance

Category: Interpersonal

A deep emotional connection and attraction between people, often involving love and affection.

Why this matters

Romance matters because "A deep emotional connection and attraction between people, often involving love and affection" can either stay abstract or become something people can observe. It affects trust, conflict, repair, and the way people leave an exchange. It appears in the small verbs around it: love, adore, and pursue. That keeps romance from becoming decoration and turns it into something a person can actually practice.

Examples in action

  • After twenty years of marriage, they maintained romance through weekly date nights, thoughtful gestures, and continuing to learn about each other's changing interests.
  • When romance is working, the action usually leaves a trace: something gets protected, clarified, repaired, strengthened, or changed.
  • Romance can also appear through its neighboring values, such as Family, Self-Compassion, and Love, when the situation asks someone to love with more intention and precision.

How to practice

  • Name the pressure that makes romance harder, then choose one action that answers that pressure directly.
  • Make the value visible to one other person through a choice they could actually notice.
  • After acting, ask whether someone else could have observed romance in what you actually did.
  • When the situation gets blurry, return to the action words: love, adore, pursue, and cherish.

Frequently asked questions

What does romance mean as a value?
As a value, romance points to A deep emotional connection and attraction between people, often involving love and affection. You can usually recognize it through concrete actions such as love, adore, pursue, and cherish.
How do I practice romance?
To practice romance, pick one of its verbs, such as "pursue", and apply it to a specific conversation, decision, routine, or repair.
What values are related to romance?
Family, Self-Compassion, and Love are useful companions to romance. They show where this value may need support, balance, or clearer boundaries.
