Selflessness

Category: Interpersonal

Putting the needs and feelings of others before your own and helping them without expecting anything in return.

Why this matters

Selflessness turns "Putting the needs and feelings of others before your own and helping them without expecting anything in return" into a lived value by giving it a form other people can notice. It becomes real in the quality of attention someone brings to another person. It often shows up through the willingness to give before the situation is neat or fully rewarded. Without that behavioral edge, selflessness can stay vague even when the word feels important.

Examples in action

  • The volunteer showed selflessness by spending her vacation building homes in disaster-affected areas rather than taking a personal holiday.
  • One small example is a relational choice: using "help" to make selflessness observable in one decision.
  • A repeated sign of selflessness is the willingness to serve even when the reward is delayed, private, or hard to measure.

How to practice

  • Use "give" as the test: what would be different by the end of the day if that action really happened?
  • After acting, ask whether someone else could have observed selflessness in what you actually did.
  • When the situation gets blurry, return to the action words: give, serve, help, and support.
  • Turn the definition into a sentence that starts with "Today I will..." and includes one concrete behavior.

Frequently asked questions

What does selflessness mean as a value?
Selflessness becomes a value when Putting the needs and feelings of others before your own and helping them without expecting anything in return starts influencing what someone chooses, refuses, repairs, or repeats.
How do I practice selflessness?
Choose a situation where the value has been mostly theoretical. Then make it visible through one action connected to give, serve, help, and support.
What values are related to selflessness?
Selflessness often connects with Benevolence, Kindness, and Philanthropy. Those values can support selflessness, sharpen its meaning, or reveal a tension worth noticing.
