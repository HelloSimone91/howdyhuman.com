Commitment

Category: Core Values

Being dedicated to doing what you said you would do and supporting others in achieving their goals.

Why this matters

Commitment matters because the idea of "Being dedicated to doing what you said you would do and supporting others in achieving their goals" is only trustworthy when it changes behavior. It usually matters most when a shortcut would be easier than the stated standard. You can often see it in the decision to dedicate, especially when nobody is forcing that choice. This gives commitment enough specificity to be noticed in a conversation, routine, decision, or repair.

Examples in action

  • Despite numerous obstacles, she showed commitment to the literacy program by volunteering every week for five years, helping hundreds learn to read.
  • In a specific decision, commitment becomes easier to recognize when the person can name what they are choosing to persist and what that action serves.
  • When commitment is working, the action usually leaves a trace: something gets protected, clarified, repaired, strengthened, or changed.

How to practice

  • Notice where you talk about commitment more easily than you practice it, then choose one concrete verb to close that gap.
  • Name the pressure that makes commitment harder, then choose one action that answers that pressure directly.
  • Use "dedicate" as the test: what would be different by the end of the day if that action really happened?
  • Compare commitment with Mission, Work Ethic, and Approachability; notice where they support each other and where they ask for different actions.

Frequently asked questions

What does commitment mean as a value?
Commitment is not only a word for Being dedicated to doing what you said you would do and supporting others in achieving their goals; it is also a pattern of behavior shaped by actions like dedicate, engage, persist, and adhere.
How do I practice commitment?
Make commitment practical by pairing it with a small commitment: one choice to persist, one conversation to have, or one pattern to interrupt.
What values are related to commitment?
Looking at Mission, Work Ethic, and Approachability alongside commitment can make the choice in front of someone more precise.
