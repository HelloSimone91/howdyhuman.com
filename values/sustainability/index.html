Sustainability

Category: Social

The ability to meet our needs today without harming the ability of future generations to meet theirs.

Why this matters

Sustainability becomes useful when "The ability to meet our needs today without harming the ability of future generations to meet theirs" starts guiding real choices instead of staying in private intention. As a social value, it reaches beyond individual preference. This is why the verb map matters: maintain, conserve, and preserve make sustainability easier to spot in real life. In that sense, sustainability is less about claiming the word and more about making one observable choice.

Examples in action

  • The building was designed with sustainability in mind, using renewable energy, water reclamation systems, and materials with minimal environmental impact.
  • In practice, sustainability can show up in a group decision where the outcome affects more than one person: someone starts with the action behind "maintain" instead of leaving the value as a private intention.
  • In a specific decision, sustainability becomes easier to recognize when the person can name what they are choosing to preserve and what that action serves.

How to practice

  • Look for the place where sustainability is being admired but not enacted, then choose the smallest useful action.
  • Use the phrase "The ability to meet our needs today without harming the ability of future generations to meet theirs" as a check: what action would make that definition visible today?
  • Notice where you talk about sustainability more easily than you practice it, then choose one concrete verb to close that gap.
  • Ask what sustainability would change in the next conversation, errand, decision, or repair.

Frequently asked questions

What does sustainability mean as a value?
Sustainability describes The ability to meet our needs today without harming the ability of future generations to meet theirs. The important question is what someone does with that meaning when a real choice is in front of them.
How do I practice sustainability?
Look for a place where sustainability is already being tested. Use "conserve" as the next action, then notice what changed afterward.
What values are related to sustainability?
Sustainability shares territory with Authenticity, Discipline, and Health, but each value points attention toward a slightly different action.
