Initiative

Category: Growth

Taking action to make things better or to start new projects without waiting for others to do it first.

Why this matters

Initiative becomes useful when "Taking action to make things better or to start new projects without waiting for others to do it first" starts guiding real choices instead of staying in private intention. It becomes visible through adjustment, repetition, and honest feedback. This is why the verb map matters: introduce, establish, and create make initiative easier to spot in real life. In that sense, initiative is less about claiming the word and more about making one observable choice.

Examples in action

  • Noticing that new employees struggled to navigate company systems, she took initiative by creating a comprehensive onboarding guide without being asked.
  • Instead of treating initiative as an identity label, someone might practice it by choosing the action behind "establish", naming what it protects, and noticing what it interrupts.
  • Another example is choosing "create" in a small but visible way before the value has become easy or automatic.

How to practice

  • Write down the smallest possible version of the action behind "establish" and use it to resist the wish to already be good at it.
  • Ask what initiative would change in the next conversation, errand, decision, or repair.
  • Choose one related value, such as Networking, Discipline, and Legacy, and decide whether the moment needs that value, initiative, or both.
  • Use the phrase "Taking action to make things better or to start new projects without waiting for others to do it first" as a check: what action would make that definition visible today?

Frequently asked questions

What does initiative mean as a value?
Initiative describes Taking action to make things better or to start new projects without waiting for others to do it first. The important question is what someone does with that meaning when a real choice is in front of them.
How do I practice initiative?
Look for a place where initiative is already being tested. Use "establish" as the next action, then notice what changed afterward.
What values are related to initiative?
