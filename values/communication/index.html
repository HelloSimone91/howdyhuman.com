Communication

Category: Interpersonal

Sharing information clearly and respectfully with others to ensure understanding and connection.

Why this matters

Communication becomes visible when a person treats "Sharing information clearly and respectfully with others to ensure understanding and connection" as a standard for action, not just a belief. As an interpersonal value, it shapes the space between people. The value is easier to recognize when the action is specific: to express, to share, or to explain. For communication, the practical test is whether the next choice becomes more honest, more intentional, or more aligned.

Examples in action

  • The manager practiced effective communication by explaining project goals clearly, listening to team concerns, and providing regular updates on progress.
  • Another example is choosing "explain" in a small but visible way before the value has become easy or automatic.
  • One small example is a relational choice: using "explain" to make communication observable in one decision.

How to practice

  • Ask what communication would change in the next conversation, errand, decision, or repair.
  • Use "express" as the test: what would be different by the end of the day if that action really happened?
  • Compare communication with Authenticity, Beauty, and Fine Art; notice where they support each other and where they ask for different actions.
  • Reread the opening example and name the exact behavior that made communication visible.

Frequently asked questions

What does communication mean as a value?
Communication is the value of treating "Sharing information clearly and respectfully with others to ensure understanding and connection" as something to practice, not just something to believe. Its behavioral signals include express, share, explain, and clarify.
How do I practice communication?
Use communication as a question before acting: what would it look like to express here? Then do the smallest honest version of that action.
What values are related to communication?
Communication can overlap with Authenticity, Beauty, and Fine Art. Comparing them helps keep the value specific instead of letting it blur into a general good intention.
