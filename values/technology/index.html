Technology

Category: Growth

The use of scientific knowledge and tools to solve problems and improve lives.

Why this matters

Technology becomes useful when "The use of scientific knowledge and tools to solve problems and improve lives" starts guiding real choices instead of staying in private intention. It becomes visible through adjustment, repetition, and honest feedback. This is why the verb map matters: develop, utilize, and apply make technology easier to spot in real life. In that sense, technology is less about claiming the word and more about making one observable choice.

Examples in action

  • The organization leveraged technology to connect isolated elderly individuals with healthcare providers and family members during the pandemic.
  • Instead of treating technology as an identity label, someone might practice it by choosing the action behind "utilize", naming what it protects, and noticing what it interrupts.
  • Another example is choosing "apply" in a small but visible way before the value has become easy or automatic.

How to practice

  • Write down the smallest possible version of the action behind "utilize" and use it to resist the wish to already be good at it.
  • Ask what technology would change in the next conversation, errand, decision, or repair.
  • Choose one related value, such as Capitalism, Creativity, and Discipline, and decide whether the moment needs that value, technology, or both.
  • Use the phrase "The use of scientific knowledge and tools to solve problems and improve lives" as a check: what action would make that definition visible today?
