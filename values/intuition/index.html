Intuition

Category: Mindset

The ability to understand something immediately, without needing to think about it or use logic.

Why this matters

Intuition matters because "The ability to understand something immediately, without needing to think about it or use logic" can either stay abstract or become something people can observe. It often appears before the outward action, in the story someone chooses to believe. It appears in the small verbs around it: sense, feel, and perceive. That keeps intuition from becoming decoration and turns it into something a person can actually practice.

Examples in action

  • The experienced doctor used her intuition to investigate symptoms that seemed minor but suggested a more serious underlying condition.
  • One small example is a attention-shaping choice: using "perceive" to make intuition observable in one decision.
  • A repeated sign of intuition is the willingness to feel even when the reward is delayed, private, or hard to measure.

How to practice

  • Compare intuition with Empathy, Beauty, and Wisdom; notice where they support each other and where they ask for different actions.
  • When the situation gets blurry, return to the action words: sense, feel, perceive, and know.
  • Turn the definition into a sentence that starts with "Today I will..." and includes one concrete behavior.
  • Make the value visible to one other person through a choice they could actually notice.

Frequently asked questions

What does intuition mean as a value?
As a value, intuition points to The ability to understand something immediately, without needing to think about it or use logic. You can usually recognize it through concrete actions such as sense, feel, perceive, and know.
How do I practice intuition?
To practice intuition, pick one of its verbs, such as "perceive", and apply it to a specific conversation, decision, routine, or repair.
What values are related to intuition?
Empathy, Beauty, and Wisdom are useful companions to intuition. They show where this value may need support, balance, or clearer boundaries.
