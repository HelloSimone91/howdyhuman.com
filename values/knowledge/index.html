Knowledge

Category: Growth

Understanding and using information, facts, and skills gained through experience or education.

Why this matters

Knowledge matters when "Understanding and using information, facts, and skills gained through experience or education" has to become more than a sentence someone agrees with. As a growth value, it asks for practice rather than performance. It becomes concrete when someone chooses to learn and lets that action carry the value. That is where the value earns trust: in the gap between what someone says matters and what they actually do.

Examples in action

  • The mentor shared knowledge accumulated over decades in the field, helping new professionals avoid common pitfalls and accelerate their development.
  • In practice, knowledge can show up while someone is learning, revising, or trying again after feedback: someone starts with the action behind "learn" instead of leaving the value as a private intention.
  • In a specific decision, knowledge becomes easier to recognize when the person can name what they are choosing to understand and what that action serves.

How to practice

  • Find one real situation while someone is learning, revising, or trying again after feedback, then choose one action connected to "learn".
  • Notice where you talk about knowledge more easily than you practice it, then choose one concrete verb to close that gap.
  • Ask what knowledge would change in the next conversation, errand, decision, or repair.
  • Choose one related value, such as Curiosity, Growth, and History, and decide whether the moment needs that value, knowledge, or both.

Frequently asked questions

What does knowledge mean as a value?
Knowledge means Understanding and using information, facts, and skills gained through experience or education. It becomes a lived value when someone uses actions like learn, study, understand, and research to make that meaning visible in a real situation.
How do I practice knowledge?
Start with a situation where the wish to already be good at it is likely to take over. Then choose a small action connected to "study" that makes knowledge visible.
What values are related to knowledge?
Curiosity, Growth, and History sit close to knowledge in the dictionary. They help frame the situations where this value becomes useful or complicated.
