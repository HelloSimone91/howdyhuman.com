Tolerance

Category: Interpersonal

Respecting and accepting differences in others, even when you don't agree with them.

Why this matters

Tolerance gives shape to "Respecting and accepting differences in others, even when you don't agree with them" by asking what a person is willing to do when the moment gets specific. It becomes real in the quality of attention someone brings to another person. Instead of asking whether someone "has" tolerance, the better question is whether they accept when it counts. The most useful question is not whether tolerance sounds good, but where it changes the next behavior.

Examples in action

  • The community practiced tolerance by creating spaces where people of different faiths could share their traditions and learn from each other respectfully.
  • Tolerance can be practiced quietly, too, through the choice to respect without turning the action into proof of identity.
  • Instead of treating tolerance as an identity label, someone might practice it by choosing the action behind "respect", naming what it protects, and noticing what it interrupts.

How to practice

  • Turn the definition into a sentence that starts with "Today I will..." and includes one concrete behavior.
  • Find one real situation inside a conversation where another person has something at stake, then choose one action connected to "accept".
  • Write down the smallest possible version of the action behind "respect" and use it to resist defensiveness.
  • Pick one verb from the map, such as "accommodate", and use it in a situation small enough to finish today.

Frequently asked questions

What does tolerance mean as a value?
Tolerance names the commitment behind Respecting and accepting differences in others, even when you don't agree with them. The value becomes easier to see when someone chooses to accept, respect, or accommodate under real constraints.
How do I practice tolerance?
Practice begins by reducing tolerance to one doable behavior. Choose a moment, choose a verb like "accept", and follow through before overexplaining it.
What values are related to tolerance?
Values near tolerance include Accountability, Ambiguity, and Forgiveness. They are worth comparing when a situation feels morally or emotionally layered.
