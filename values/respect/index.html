Respect

Category: Interpersonal

Treating others with kindness and valuing their feelings, opinions, and rights.

Why this matters

Respect becomes useful when "Treating others with kindness and valuing their feelings, opinions, and rights" starts guiding real choices instead of staying in private intention. As an interpersonal value, it shapes the space between people. This is why the verb map matters: honor, admire, and value make respect easier to spot in real life. In that sense, respect is less about claiming the word and more about making one observable choice.

Examples in action

  • The debate moderator ensured an atmosphere of respect by establishing clear guidelines for discussion and intervening when participants became dismissive.
  • In practice, respect can show up inside a conversation where another person has something at stake: someone starts with the action behind "honor" instead of leaving the value as a private intention.
  • In a specific decision, respect becomes easier to recognize when the person can name what they are choosing to value and what that action serves.

How to practice

  • Look for the place where respect is being admired but not enacted, then choose the smallest useful action.
  • Use the phrase "Treating others with kindness and valuing their feelings, opinions, and rights" as a check: what action would make that definition visible today?
  • Notice where you talk about respect more easily than you practice it, then choose one concrete verb to close that gap.
  • Ask what respect would change in the next conversation, errand, decision, or repair.

Frequently asked questions

What does respect mean as a value?
Respect describes Treating others with kindness and valuing their feelings, opinions, and rights. The important question is what someone does with that meaning when a real choice is in front of them.
How do I practice respect?
Look for a place where respect is already being tested. Use "admire" as the next action, then notice what changed afterward.
What values are related to respect?
Respect shares territory with Authenticity, Celebration, and Integrity, but each value points attention toward a slightly different action.
