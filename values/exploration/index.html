Exploration

Category: Growth

The act of searching for new places, ideas, or experiences to learn and discover.

Why this matters

Exploration matters because "The act of searching for new places, ideas, or experiences to learn and discover" can either stay abstract or become something people can observe. It becomes visible through adjustment, repetition, and honest feedback. It appears in the small verbs around it: discover, investigate, and examine. That keeps exploration from becoming decoration and turns it into something a person can actually practice.

Examples in action

  • The company dedicated time each week for employees to pursue exploration of topics outside their usual responsibilities, leading to innovative solutions.
  • When exploration is working, the action usually leaves a trace: something gets protected, clarified, repaired, strengthened, or changed.
  • Exploration can also appear through its neighboring values, such as Adventure, Curiosity, and Knowledge, when the situation asks someone to discover with more intention and precision.

How to practice

  • Name the pressure that makes exploration harder, then choose one action that answers that pressure directly.
  • Make the value visible to one other person through a choice they could actually notice.
  • After acting, ask whether someone else could have observed exploration in what you actually did.
  • When the situation gets blurry, return to the action words: discover, investigate, examine, and study.

Frequently asked questions

What does exploration mean as a value?
As a value, exploration points to The act of searching for new places, ideas, or experiences to learn and discover. You can usually recognize it through concrete actions such as discover, investigate, examine, and study.
How do I practice exploration?
To practice exploration, pick one of its verbs, such as "examine", and apply it to a specific conversation, decision, routine, or repair.
What values are related to exploration?
Adventure, Curiosity, and Knowledge are useful companions to exploration. They show where this value may need support, balance, or clearer boundaries.
