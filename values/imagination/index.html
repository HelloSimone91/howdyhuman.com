Imagination

Category: Mindset

The ability to create ideas or images in your mind that are not present in the real world.

Why this matters

Imagination becomes visible when a person treats "The ability to create ideas or images in your mind that are not present in the real world" as a standard for action, not just a belief. As a mindset value, it changes how pressure, choice, and uncertainty are interpreted. The value is easier to recognize when the action is specific: to envision, to create, or to invent. For imagination, the practical test is whether the next choice becomes more honest, more intentional, or more aligned.

Examples in action

  • The architect used imagination to design a building that not only met functional requirements but also inspired wonder and connected with its natural surroundings.
  • Another example is choosing "invent" in a small but visible way before the value has become easy or automatic.
  • One small example is a attention-shaping choice: using "invent" to make imagination observable in one decision.

How to practice

  • Ask what imagination would change in the next conversation, errand, decision, or repair.
  • Use "envision" as the test: what would be different by the end of the day if that action really happened?
  • Compare imagination with Foresight, Hope, and Optimism; notice where they support each other and where they ask for different actions.
  • Reread the opening example and name the exact behavior that made imagination visible.

Frequently asked questions

What does imagination mean as a value?
Imagination is the value of treating "The ability to create ideas or images in your mind that are not present in the real world" as something to practice, not just something to believe. Its behavioral signals include envision, create, invent, and conceive.
How do I practice imagination?
Use imagination as a question before acting: what would it look like to envision here? Then do the smallest honest version of that action.
What values are related to imagination?
Imagination can overlap with Foresight, Hope, and Optimism. Comparing them helps keep the value specific instead of letting it blur into a general good intention.
