Authenticity

Category: Core Values

Being true to oneself and honest with others, showing real feelings and beliefs.

Why this matters

Authenticity becomes visible when a person treats "Being true to oneself and honest with others, showing real feelings and beliefs" as a standard for action, not just a belief. As a core value, it works like an inner standard for repeated choices. The value is easier to recognize when the action is specific: to express, to reveal, or to represent. For authenticity, the practical test is whether the next choice becomes more honest, more intentional, or more aligned.

Examples in action

  • During the job interview, she showed authenticity by honestly discussing both her strengths and areas where she needed growth.
  • Another example is choosing "represent" in a small but visible way before the value has become easy or automatic.
  • One small example is a standard-setting choice: using "represent" to make authenticity observable in one decision.

How to practice

  • Ask what authenticity would change in the next conversation, errand, decision, or repair.
  • Use "express" as the test: what would be different by the end of the day if that action really happened?
  • Compare authenticity with Beauty, Communication, and Fine Art; notice where they support each other and where they ask for different actions.
  • Reread the opening example and name the exact behavior that made authenticity visible.

Frequently asked questions

What does authenticity mean as a value?
Authenticity is the value of treating "Being true to oneself and honest with others, showing real feelings and beliefs" as something to practice, not just something to believe. Its behavioral signals include express, reveal, represent, and embody.
How do I practice authenticity?
Use authenticity as a question before acting: what would it look like to express here? Then do the smallest honest version of that action.
What values are related to authenticity?
Authenticity can overlap with Beauty, Communication, and Fine Art. Comparing them helps keep the value specific instead of letting it blur into a general good intention.
