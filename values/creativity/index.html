Creativity

Category: Growth

Using imagination and original ideas to create something new or solve problems.

Why this matters

Creativity gives shape to "Using imagination and original ideas to create something new or solve problems" by asking what a person is willing to do when the moment gets specific. It belongs to the moments where trying again matters more than already being good. Instead of asking whether someone "has" creativity, the better question is whether they invent when it counts. The most useful question is not whether creativity sounds good, but where it changes the next behavior.

Examples in action

  • The teacher showed creativity by designing a classroom economy that made learning math concepts engaging through real-world applications.
  • Creativity can be practiced quietly, too, through the choice to imagine without turning the action into proof of identity.
  • Instead of treating creativity as an identity label, someone might practice it by choosing the action behind "imagine", naming what it protects, and noticing what it interrupts.

How to practice

  • Turn the definition into a sentence that starts with "Today I will..." and includes one concrete behavior.
  • Find one real situation while someone is learning, revising, or trying again after feedback, then choose one action connected to "invent".
  • Write down the smallest possible version of the action behind "imagine" and use it to resist the wish to already be good at it.
  • Pick one verb from the map, such as "design", and use it in a situation small enough to finish today.

Frequently asked questions

What does creativity mean as a value?
Creativity names the commitment behind Using imagination and original ideas to create something new or solve problems. The value becomes easier to see when someone chooses to invent, imagine, or design under real constraints.
How do I practice creativity?
Practice begins by reducing creativity to one doable behavior. Choose a moment, choose a verb like "invent", and follow through before overexplaining it.
What values are related to creativity?
Values near creativity include Imagination, Hope, and Vision. They are worth comparing when a situation feels morally or emotionally layered.
